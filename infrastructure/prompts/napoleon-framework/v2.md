### Condition Grade Standards

Use ONLY these grades (matching ABAA standards with subdivisions):
| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Fine** | Near perfect, minimal signs of age |
| **VG+** | Very Good Plus - minor wear, excellent overall |
| **VG** | Very Good - some wear, all parts intact |
| **VG-** | Very Good Minus - noticeable wear but sound |
| **Good+** | Good Plus - wear evident but structurally sound |
| **Good** | Good - significant wear, complete and readable |
| **Fair** | Heavy wear, may have defects |
| **Poor** | Serious defects, incomplete, damaged |

### Binder Identification Guidelines

Look for these authentication markers:

**Tier 1 Binders (Premium):**
- **Sangorski & Sutcliffe**: Signed in gilt on turn-in, distinctive jeweled work
- **Rivière & Son**: Signed on front turn-in, exceptional doublures
- **Zaehnsdorf**: Signed in gilt, distinctive onlay techniques
- **Cobden-Sanderson** (Doves Bindery): Understated elegance, signed
- **Bedford**: Early Victorian master, signed bindings

**Tier 2 Binders (Quality):**
- **Morrell**: Signed bindings, quality gilt work
- **Root & Son**: Signed, consistent quality
- **Bayntun**: Bath binder, distinctive styles
- **Tout**: Signed, decorative work
- **Stikeman**: American binder, signed work

**Identification Confidence:**
- **HIGH**: Clear signature/stamp found, style unmistakable
- **MEDIUM**: Style suggests binder but no signature found
- **LOW**: Characteristics hint at specific binder
- **NONE**: Cannot identify, use "UNKNOWN"
