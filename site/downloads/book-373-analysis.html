Historical Significance

The Text: Chaboulon's Hundred Days Chronicle

Baron Chaboulon served as Napoleon's Special Secretary during the Hundred Days - the dramatic 110-day period from Napoleon's escape from Elba through Waterloo to his second abdication. Published in 1820 while Napoleon still lived at Saint Helena, these memoirs offered immediate historical testimony from Napoleon's inner circle.

The Publisher: John Murray II

John Murray II (1778-1843) was Britain's most prestigious publisher by 1820, publishing Lord Byron, Jane Austen, Sir Walter Scott, and Samuel Taylor Coleridge. A Murray imprint signaled serious historical literature.

The Binding: Sangorski & Sutcliffe

Founded October 1, 1901, Sangorski & Sutcliffe became "the most famous and fashionable bookbinders in England" within five years. Their most famous creation - "The Great Omar" Rubaiyat with over 1,050 jewels - sank with the Titanic, cementing their legendary status.
